Motivation

Modern neutron scattering -> large and complex data

Scientists at user facilites (ORNL SNS) have limited time and often low proficiency with 3D visualization tools.

Most analysis is conducted after leaving the facility, which is not productive.

  • A lot has changed since the early days of neutron scattering experiments when scientists manually configured instruments and recorded neutron counts by hand.
  • Today the data collected at scientific facilities such as the ORNL Spallation Neutron Source are large and complex.
  • To make matters worse, time is expensive and limited at these facilities.
  • The most efficient use of time would be to analyze the data at the facility so that new insight could be used to improve the experiment.
  • However, most scientists are not proficient with the complex 3D visualization and analysis tools required to work with the data.
  • Consequently, most analysis is conducted after leaving the facility, which precludes iterative feedback into the experiment.

An Emerging Solution

An immersive visual analytics system could lower the barriers to advanced visualization and analysis and unlock the full potential of scientific analysis.

More intuitive interactions with complex data.

Timely analysis results can be used to modify experimental parameters.

  • We partnered with scientists at the ORNL SNS to try a new approach that focused on the idea of immersive visual analysis.
  • We felt that a VR solution could lower the barriers to advanced visualization and analysis, which in turn could allow scientists to navigate and investigate the data in a more timely fashion.
  • Our initial trials with an immersive visual analytics showed much promise and has helped us develop a more comprehensive vision to the future of such an approach.
